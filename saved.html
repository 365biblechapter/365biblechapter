Are You Saved?

The Bible asks us to examine ourselves to see whether we are in the faith (2 Corinthians 13:5). But salvation is not found in religion, rituals, or good deeds. It is found in a person: Jesus Christ. And the true Gospel is found in one place: 1 Corinthians 15:1–4.

“Christ died for our sins according to the scriptures; and that he was buried, and that he rose again the third day according to the scriptures.”
1 Corinthians 15:3–4

This is not about what you do for God. It's about what Jesus already did for you. He shed His blood on the cross to fully pay for your sins. Then He was buried, and He rose again, proving He is Lord.

The Gospel Has 5 Truths:

  • Christ died
  • For our sins
  • Was buried
  • Rose again
  • According to the Scriptures

That’s it. No more. No less. The blood of Jesus is the only payment God accepts.

How Can I Be Saved?

Salvation is by grace through faith, not by works, church membership, or even water baptism. The Bible says:

“For by grace are ye saved through faith... not of works, lest any man should boast.”
Ephesians 2:8–9

You are saved the moment you place your full trust—not in yourself—but in the finished work of Jesus Christ and His shed blood.

Faith in His Blood

The Bible says we are saved:

“Through faith in His blood...”
Romans 3:25

That blood was sprinkled on the mercy seat in Heaven, and it still speaks today: mercy, forgiveness, and eternal life.
